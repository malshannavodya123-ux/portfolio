Mapping Urban Brightness Across Sri Lanka Using VIIRS Nighttime Satellite Data

Mapping urban brightness across Sri Lanka is essential for understanding patterns of human activity, infrastructure development, and economic growth. Nighttime satellite imagery from the VIIRS sensor provides a reliable, consistent source of data to evaluate artificial light emissions, which act as a strong proxy for urbanization intensity. This study focuses on analyzing Sri Lanka’s 2022 nightlight radiance to identify spatial variations in brightness across districts, highlighting the influence of population density, industrial zones, and transportation networks. Using Google Earth Engine (GEE), annual and monthly radiance composites were processed to detect areas of concentrated illumination. The analysis reveals significantly higher brightness around Colombo, the Western Province, and key coastal cities such as Galle and Jaffna, reflecting dense urban development and active economic centers. In contrast, interior districts and the eastern provinces show markedly lower radiance, indicating sparse settlements and limited infrastructure. Monthly radiance trends further illustrate seasonal fluctuations in lighting intensity, influenced by weather conditions and energy-use patterns. The resulting nightlight maps provide valuable insights for urban planners, policymakers, and disaster management authorities by helping track urban expansion, identify underserved regions, and support evidence-based development strategies across Sri Lanka.
