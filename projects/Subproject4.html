Data Collection for Sri Lanka’s Climate Change and Development Report

This job role involves supporting the World Bank’s Liveable Cities deep-dive analysis for Sri Lanka’s Climate Change and Development Report (CCDR), with a specific focus on the Colombo Metropolitan Area. The role is responsible for identifying, requesting, and coordinating the collection of high-quality geospatial and urban datasets required for detailed urban climate and development analysis. The responsibilities include conducting literature reviews and open-data stocktaking to identify data gaps, and formally requesting spatial datasets in TIF and Shapefile (.shp) formats from relevant institutions and stakeholders. The datasets support analysis of urban dynamics, economic productivity, climate vulnerability and hazards, greenhouse gas emissions, and waste management challenges within the metropolitan region. Key data themes under this role include current land use plans, transport infrastructure networks (roads, railways, and highways), coverage of sewage and wastewater systems, distribution of underserved settlements such as slums and shanties, blue–green infrastructure networks, and flood impact zones and disaster risk reduction plans. The role ensures that all data are spatially accurate, consistent, and suitable for integration into GIS-based analytical workflows. Overall, this position plays a critical role in enabling evidence-based urban planning and climate resilience assessment, directly contributing to national-level climate and development reporting for Sri Lanka
