About Me

I’m Malshan Navodya Karunarathna, a dedicated Geospatial Technology professional graduated with a BSc. (Hons) from the University of Sri Jayewardenepura. My foundation lies in utilizing advanced GIS and Remote Sensing technologies to solve real-world spatial challenges, focusing on data analysis, mapping, and project execution across public service and environmental sectors.

Currently, I drive critical spatial analysis initiatives as a Research Assistant specializing in GIS at the Coconut Research Institute (CRI). My work bridges advanced Remote Sensing analysis with vital field-level resource management. I architect and maintain geospatial data workflows, automate the processing of satellite imagery for detailed land suitability modeling, and deliver high-impact visual outputs and decision-support systems crucial for coconut cultivation and environmental assessments.

My core expertise lies in the Esri ArcGIS suite, where I have orchestrated implementations—from geospatial data management to GIS-based custom web application development. I am also well-versed in integrating cloud technologies to enhance performance, scalability, and cost-efficiency for enterprise-level GIS deployments.

This expertise is backed by diverse practical experience, including contributing geospatial data to the Sri Lanka’s Climate Change and Development Report (CCDR) for the World Bank and successfully executing a GIS-based Light Pole problem complaint system. Through past roles at the Land Reform Commission and as a GIS Technician, I have refined my ability to manage large geodatabases, integrate real-world field data, and deliver detailed mapping inventories for national projects.

This expertise extends into the freelance market, where I operate as a GIS Analyst on platforms like Upwork. This freelance work has sharpened my skills in project scoping, client communication, and delivering precise, customized geospatial solutions across varied industries, from boundary mapping to data visualization projects, all while adhering to strict deadlines and quality standards.

Beyond technical delivery, my tenure as the President of the Youth Mapper Chapter - USJ (UN Mappers) demonstrates proven leadership, effective communication, and project management skills. I am committed to leveraging my comprehensive geospatial and analytical skills to secure challenging roles that drive data-informed decisions and contribute meaningfully to modern infrastructure and societal success.
